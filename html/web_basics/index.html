Looking into a day in the life of a NY house cat

When the windows turn on and my humans have not yet stirred I must act. On a good day my humans will wake to make themselves a crushed hot bean drink, it smells of dirt and occasionally another cat's poop. But on the days when the windows turn on and they still lay there I must rub their faces or they will die.

After the humans have consumed their hot bean drink I might get scratches from my human.

On good days it continues. Ooooh, aaaaaaahh...

But then the abomination...

The sloppy collision of dog and machine! It traps you into its massive blue scoop and tries to feed. The dangerous aspects of the beast are.

  1. Gaping jaws
  2. Monstrous scoop
  3. Its thirst for blood
If am careful I can hide in the human's sleeping room. There I wait for their return...

The small human has grown from larva to miniature. She still smells of milk, but tends to be very... grabby... If I wait until she slumbers I can lick the dried milk from her cheeks.

The remainder of my day will usually contain all if not some of the following.

  • -Hiding under the bed
  • -Chasing the mysterious red light
  • -Swatting pen caps
  • -Sleep

Until tomorrow...
